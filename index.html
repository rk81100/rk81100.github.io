<p> University of Georgia has many amazing things to offer its students, including student housing. Not only does this school house over 27,000 students, but it provides housing that proves better than many other schools. Whether you're an freshman, a parent looking for a place for your child, or simpy curious, this website will compare and contrast the housing options available at the University of Georgia. </p>

<table id="t01" class="center" border="1">

<tbody>

<tr>

<th>Building</th>

<th>Location</th>

<th>Pros</th>

<th>Cons</th>

</tr>

<tr>

<td>East Campus Village</td>

<td>East Campus Road</td>

<td> This option is close to the REC workout center, houses from 2 to 4 roomates per room,and includes private bed and bath. A meal plan is also available with this option.</td>

<td>ECV is more expensive than other buildings. It houses mostly athletes, and students often complain about the distance from other housing and classes being too far.</td>

</tr>

<tr>

<td>Brumby Hall</td>

<td>Baxter Street</td>

<td>While smaller than ECV, this building houses 2 people per room, and has community bathrooms. Many students like this building becasue of the social aspect, given how popular this dorm is for freshmen.</td>

<td>Students have complained about the size of the rooms, and the bathrooms being unsanitary.</td>

</tr>

<tr>

<td>Payne Hall</td>

<td>East Campus Road</td>

<td>Many people like Payne hall because it has a good combonation of everything. Located right by the stadium and most freshmen classes, students enjoy a bigger room than brumby, but in a more dynamic location.</td>

<td>Cons to Payne hall include the lack of social aspects. Payne is often the last option for students, therefor not many are hosued there. Also, there have been recent complaints of mold in this old building.</td>

</tr>

</tbody>

</table>

<p>Laid out above is just 3 of the many options available to freshmen for housing. I have included two drastically different halls, ECV and Brumby, and then one building that has attributes of both. My freshman year, I lived in ECV and experienced both the pros and the cons listed above. I longed to be closer to my friends and classes, but was able to take advantage of the school's workout center and dining hall near by. All in all, the choice a student makes when chosing housing comes down to preference, and I hope this table helps make that decision easier.</p>
